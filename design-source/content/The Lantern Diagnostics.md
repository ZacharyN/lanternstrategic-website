## The Operational Risk Velocity Diagnostic

**The question it asks:** Which of the risks in your business are moving fast, which are moving slowly, and which kind is on your radar?

Operational risk shows up in two speeds. Fast risks announce themselves — a key employee resigns, a vendor goes down, a breach happens. Slow risks accumulate quietly — margin compresses by half a point a quarter, vendor lock-in deepens, technical debt grows. Human attention is calibrated to fast change, which leads most organizations to track fast risks closely and slow risks loosely, often without realizing it.

This diagnostic walks through five domains: people and knowledge, vendors, technology, regulatory, and financial. For each domain it asks what's moving fast and what's moving slowly. The diagnostic will surface the pattern of where you're attending well and where you're not.

_Time investment: Roughly fifteen minutes._ _**Best for** operators who are looking to understand the risks their business faces. It is an excellent starting point for those beginning to think about operational excellence._

## The Sensing Feedback Loop Diagnostic

**The question it asks:** How does your business notice when something has changed and what happens with what it notices?

Business problems are rarely invisible. The challenge most businesses face is moving from recognizing the problem to getting the right people to act on it. A bookkeeper sees the margin compressing. A long-tenured employee notices a vendor's quality slipping. A salesperson hears the same objection three times in a quarter. The signal exists, but is it reaching decision-makers?

This diagnostic looks at the feedback loops your business depends on, across five domains: customers and market, financials and operations, employees, vendors, and the broader operating environment. The diagnostic helps you examine how the signals are being generated and what happens once the signal is received. Most businesses learn well in some areas and poorly in others. The asymmetry is usually the most useful thing the diagnostic surfaces.

_Time investment: Roughly fifteen minutes._ _**Best for** operators who suspect their business has information it isn't using, or who want to think about how their organization actually learns._

## The Antifragile Diagnostic

**The question it asks:** Where does your business get stronger from stress, where does it just survive stress, and where does stress actually do damage?

Most business advice stops at resilience — the ability to absorb a shock and return to baseline. Resilience is real and valuable, but it's also not the highest bar. Some businesses, by design or by accident, use stress as a catalyst for improvement. Nassim Nicholas Taleb, who coined the term, calls this "antifragility." Two businesses lose a major client. One struggles to regain its footing; the other ends the year stronger than before. Same shock, different trajectory.

This diagnostic walks through five domains: revenue, key dependencies, operations, financial position, and people. It asks you where your business sits on the spectrum from fragile to antifragile in each domain. This diagnostic will help you begin to surface the specific places where exposure has been quietly accumulating, and where you may already have built more strength than you realize.

_Time investment: Roughly fifteen minutes._ _**Best for** operators who are interested in a structural look at where their business sits relative to uncertainty._
