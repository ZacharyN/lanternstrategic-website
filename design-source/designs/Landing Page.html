The approach

The problems that don't announce themselves.

Most of the friction in a business doesn't announce itself. It's the third meeting about the same thing. The workaround that became the process. The report that takes a full day to pull together, originally built to answer a question nobody asks anymore.

These quiet problems are the ones easiest to live with — and hardest to fix from the inside.

The friction becomes the background. The workaround becomes the process. And the cost compounds in ways nobody's tracking. Lantern Strategic is built to address the quiet problems — the ones that don't show up in a dashboard, but show up in everyone's calendar.

01

The third meeting about the same thing.

Decisions that keep coming back because no one named who actually owns them.

02

A process everyone has a workaround for.

The map and the territory drifted apart years ago. Nobody flagged it.

03

The day-long report no one reads.

Built for a question that mattered once. Still costs a day every month.

04

Recurring meetings that outlived their purpose.

The calendar fills up faster than anyone removes from it. Attention pays the bill.

Engagements

Three ways to engage.

Every business is at a different point in the conversation. Engagements are scoped to fit; pricing is project-based or retainer.

01 · Defined projects

A clear scope, a specific outcome.

Technology evaluations · AI training · focused operational assessments.

Flat fee. Defined deliverable. For when you already know what you need handled and want it handled cleanly.

When it fits — the problem is named, the scope is known.

02 · Discovery & plan

Sit with the business. Learn how it actually runs.

A defined period of listening, observing, and structuring.

We produce a plan you can execute — or hand back to us to run. For when you know something needs to change but naming it cleanly is part of the work.

When it fits — the symptom is loud, the diagnosis is not yet.

03 · Embedded expertise

Senior operations thinking, on your team.

Fractional executive support · coaching · on-site training · office hours.

A sounding board for the calls that don't fit anywhere else. For when the value is in having someone to think with, not just a deliverable to receive.

When it fits — the work is ongoing, the questions keep coming.

About

Zach Nelson — operations and technology executive.

Fifteen years working with organizations under pressure, tracking the same pattern across very different industries.

Zach's career has followed one thread: the difference between a business that scales and one that stalls is rarely strategy — it's the operational design underneath. He's supported over a hundred Right at Home and IKOR franchisees through every stage, from new owners getting started to established operators looking to exit.

He's done issue advocacy work, disaster recovery consulting, and nonprofit leadership. Along the way, he's built data infrastructure, implemented patient advocacy software, and led regional marketing operations.

A generalist by orientation and a systems thinker by training — most useful in rooms where the straightforward answers have already been tried and the real problem hasn't surfaced yet.
